About Me

I grew up in Northern New Jersey and was part of the Boy Scouts, having worked my way to Eagle Scout where I developed a keen interest in integrating different areas of life for my own personal development. I graduated Stevens Institute of Technology in 2013 with a B.S. in Computer Science and went on to work in Systems Administration for the past 5 years. In 2018 I took a look at how far web development has come and realized that it was time to reinvest in my abilities as a developer, so I enrolled in the Rutgers Coding Bootcamp for Full Stack Development and haven't looked back.

I am an experienced systems administrator with a demonstrated history of proposing solutions and translating the design into implementation. Moreover, systems administration puts tight demands on communication and time management which are not the only skills I have developed but also alleviated through automating processes. I enjoy using my background in systems administration to make informed decisions on backend development knowing the impact it has on websites, software platforms, and ultimately the end user experience. A culture of good teamwork and mutual learning harness that potential and create meaningful websites and applications. Just as in my personal life, I recognize the importance of taking a multidisciplinary approach to solving problems from the technologies, languages, and frameworks we use to teamwork, problem-solving, and good communication.

When I'm not working on websites and applications, you can usually find me enjoying the company of good friends or reading interesting books.
